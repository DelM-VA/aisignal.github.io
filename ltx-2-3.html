The open AI video model that just changed the game — LTX 2.3 explained

Lightricks just released LTX 2.3 — a fully open AI video model that generates 4K video with synchronized audio, runs on your own laptop, and costs nothing if your company earns under $10M. Here's what it means for everyone.

MM
Martin Mwanduka

8 min read · April 14, 2026

Every few months, something happens in AI that quietly reshapes what's possible. Not a press conference, not a record-breaking benchmark — just a model release that lands on GitHub and HuggingFace, and quietly makes the expensive, proprietary tools around it look unnecessary.

LTX 2.3 is one of those moments.

Released in March 2026 by Lightricks, LTX 2.3 is the latest version of their open AI video engine. It generates 4K video with synchronized audio — from a text prompt, an image, or a sound file. It runs locally on consumer hardware. It's free for most people. And it's fully open source.

Let's break down what that actually means.

First — what even is LTX?

LTX is an AI video ecosystem built by Lightricks, an Israeli tech company best known for consumer photo and video apps. But LTX isn't a consumer app — it's an AI engine designed for developers, studios, and creators who want to build serious video workflows.

Think of it as the engine underneath. You give it instructions — a text description, a starting image, or an audio file — and it generates a video. Not a slideshow, not a Ken Burns pan across a photo. Actual video, with motion, camera movement, and sound.

LTX 2.3 is the latest version of that engine, and it's the biggest upgrade yet.

What's new in version 2.3?

LTX 2 launched in October 2025 and already made waves. Version 2.3, released in March 2026, brings four upgrades that matter in practice — not just on paper.

  • Sharper visuals The entire image processing system (called the VAE) was rebuilt from scratch. The result: faces look more realistic, textures hold up at full resolution, and edges stay crisp. If you've ever generated AI video and cringed at the smeared, waxy look — this is the fix.
  • Better instruction following A new text connector means when you write "camera slowly zooms in as she turns to face the window at sunset," the model actually does that — instead of generating something loosely related to your prompt and hoping you don't notice.
  • Native portrait video For the first time, you can generate true 1080×1920 vertical video. This isn't a crop. The model was trained directly on portrait-orientation footage. That means no weird composition artifacts, no off-center subjects — just clean vertical video ready for TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts.
  • Cleaner audio The audio generation has been significantly improved. Background noise and silence gaps have been removed from the training data. More importantly, you can now use audio as the input — meaning a voice, a piece of music, or a sound effect can drive the rhythm and motion of the video itself.

Why does open source matter here?

Most powerful AI video tools — Sora, Veo, Runway — are closed systems. You use them through a website or an API. You can't see the model. You can't modify it. You can't run it locally. And you pay every time you generate something.

LTX 2.3 is different. The model weights — the actual trained intelligence behind the system — are freely available on HuggingFace. Anyone can download them, run them on their own machine, modify them, and build products with them.

This matters for three reasons:

Privacy. Your footage never leaves your machine. For studios, brands, or anyone generating sensitive content, that's not a small thing.

Cost. Once you have the model locally, generation is essentially free. No credits, no subscriptions, no per-second pricing eating into your budget.

Control. You can fine-tune the model on your own style. LTX 2.3 ships with a LoRA trainer — meaning you can teach the model to generate video in your specific visual style, with your characters, in your brand aesthetic.

Licensing note: LTX 2.3 is free for companies with under $10M in annual revenue. Companies above that threshold need a commercial license from Lightricks. Always check current terms at ltx.io before commercial deployment.

Who is this actually for?

The honest answer is: a lot of people. Here's how different groups will use this differently.

Content creators

Generate native vertical video from a text prompt. Describe your scene, pick your aspect ratio, and let the model do the work. No camera, no editing suite, no stock footage subscription.

Developers and startups

LTX 2.3 ships with an API, a ComfyUI integration, and full model weights. You can build AI video generation directly into your product without managing your own GPU infrastructure — or run it entirely on-premise if you need to.

Filmmakers and studios

4K at 50fps with synchronized audio is genuinely production-grade output. The two generation modes — Fast Flow for iteration and Pro Flow for final renders — map cleanly onto a real production workflow.

Marketers and agencies

Fast turnaround for client pitches, concept reels, and social content. The LTX Studio platform wraps the same engine in a full creative suite designed for teams who don't want to touch a command line.

The bigger picture

For the past two years, the narrative around AI video has been dominated by OpenAI's Sora and Google's Veo. Impressive, yes. But expensive, closed, and out of reach for most people building real things.

LTX 2.3 is part of a different story — one where open models close the quality gap faster than anyone expected. A year ago, open AI video looked rough compared to the commercial giants. Today, LTX 2.3 is generating 4K video at 50fps with synchronized audio, running locally on consumer hardware, for free.

The pace of this is not slowing down.

Whether you're a solo creator, a developer building a product, or a studio evaluating AI tools — LTX 2.3 is worth your attention this week. The gap between "what the big labs have" and "what anyone can use" just got a lot smaller.
